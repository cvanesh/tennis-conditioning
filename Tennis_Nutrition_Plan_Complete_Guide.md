# COMPREHENSIVE NUTRITION PLAN FOR 12-YEAR-OLD COMPETITIVE TENNIS PLAYER
## Evidence-Based Meal Templates & Guidelines

---

## **NUTRITIONAL FOUNDATION & PRINCIPLES**

### **Daily Macronutrient Targets for 12-Year-Old Tennis Player**

**Body Weight Assumption:** ~45-50 kg (100-110 lbs) - adjust proportionally

**Daily Caloric Needs:**
- **Training Days:** 2,200-2,500 kcal
- **Competition Days:** 2,400-2,600 kcal
- **Rest Days:** 1,900-2,100 kcal

**Macronutrient Distribution:**

**TRAINING DAYS:**
- Carbohydrates: 6-7 g/kg body weight = 270-350g (55-60% of calories)
- Protein: 1.6 g/kg body weight = 72-80g (15-20% of calories)
- Fats: 1.5-2 g/kg body weight = 67-100g (25-30% of calories)

**COMPETITION DAYS:**
- Carbohydrates: 7-8 g/kg body weight = 315-400g (60-65% of calories)
- Protein: 1.4-1.6 g/kg body weight = 63-80g (12-15% of calories)
- Fats: 1.2-1.5 g/kg body weight = 54-75g (20-25% of calories)

**REST DAYS:**
- Carbohydrates: 4-5 g/kg body weight = 180-250g (45-50% of calories)
- Protein: 1.6 g/kg body weight = 72-80g (20-25% of calories)
- Fats: 1.5-2 g/kg body weight = 67-100g (30-35% of calories)

## **HYDRATION GUIDELINES**

### **Daily Baseline Hydration**
- **Target:** 0.5-1 oz per pound of body weight
- **For 100 lb athlete:** 50-100 oz (6-12 cups) throughout the day
- **Urine color check:** Aim for pale straw yellow

### **Pre-Exercise Hydration**
- **2 hours before:** 16-24 oz water
- **10-20 minutes before:** 7-10 oz water

### **During Exercise**
- **Every 15-20 minutes:** 4-6 oz water
- **For sessions >60 minutes:** Sports drink with electrolytes
- **Target sodium content:** 110-240 mg per 8 oz serving

### **Post-Exercise Hydration**
- **Within 2 hours:** 16-24 oz for every pound lost
- **Best options:**
  - Water
  - Coconut water (natural electrolytes)
  - Low-fat chocolate milk (hydration + recovery)
  - Sports drinks (if multiple matches/sessions)

### **Hydration Timing Rules**
✓ Drink BEFORE feeling thirsty (thirst = already dehydrated)
✓ Spread intake throughout day, not all at once
✓ Limit caffeine (none for 12-year-olds ideally)
✓ Monitor bathroom frequency (every 2-4 hours = good)

## **MEAL TIMING FRAMEWORK**

### **Timing Relative to Training/Matches**

**PRE-TRAINING/MATCH MEALS:**
- **3-4 hours before:** Large meal (70% carbs, 30% protein, low fat)
- **2-3 hours before:** Moderate meal (70% carbs, 30% protein, low fat)
- **1-2 hours before:** Light snack (primarily carbs)
- **<1 hour before:** Very light, easily digestible (banana, crackers)

**POST-TRAINING/MATCH NUTRITION:**
- **0-30 minutes (CRITICAL WINDOW):** Recovery snack (50% carbs, 40% protein, 10% fat)
- **1-3 hours after:** Full recovery meal (balanced)

**TRAINING/MATCH TIMING ADJUSTMENTS:**
- Early morning (before 10 AM): Carb-load night before + light breakfast
- Afternoon (2-4 PM): Standard pre-match meal 2-3 hours before
- Evening (after 5 PM): Lighter pre-match meal, focus on recovery after

## **PRACTICAL GUIDELINES**

### **Food Preparation & Timing Rules**

**SOLID vs. LIQUID Intake:**

**Use SOLID FOODS when:**
- 3+ hours before training/match
- Post-training meals (1+ hours after)
- Regular meals throughout day
- Better satiety and sustained energy

**Use LIQUID/SHAKES when:**
- 30-60 minutes before training (if needed)
- Immediate post-training (0-30 min window)
- Between multiple matches
- Easier/faster digestion
- When appetite is suppressed
- Quick nutrient delivery

### **Timing Before Physical Activity**

**3-4 hours before:**
- Full meal, normal portions
- Can include moderate fat and fiber
- Examples: Regular lunch/dinner options

**2-3 hours before:**
- Moderate meal
- Lower fat, moderate fiber
- Examples: Pasta dishes, rice bowls, sandwiches

**1-2 hours before:**
- Light snack
- Low fat, low fiber, high simple carbs
- Examples: Toast, banana, crackers, energy bar

**<1 hour before:**
- Very light if anything
- Quick-digesting simple carbs only
- Examples: Banana, few crackers, sports drink

### **Ingredient Quality Guidelines**

**PRIORITIZE:**
✓ Whole grains over refined (except pre-match)
✓ Wild-caught fish over farm-raised
✓ Organic chicken when possible
✓ Fresh fruits and vegetables
✓ Natural nut butters (minimal ingredients)
✓ Greek yogurt over regular
✓ Brown rice over white (except pre-match)

**AVOID/LIMIT:**
✗ Processed meats (hot dogs, deli meat with nitrates)
✗ High-sugar cereals
✗ Fried foods
✗ High-fat dairy (except post-training)
✗ Artificial sweeteners
✗ Energy drinks
✗ Sodas

**MATCH DAY EXCEPTIONS:**
- Can use white bread/rice for easier digestion
- Simple carbs are okay pre-match
- Focus on what settles well in stomach

### **Supplement Considerations (12-Year-Old)**

**RECOMMENDED:**
- Multivitamin (if diet gaps exist)
- Vitamin D (especially if limited sun exposure)
- Omega-3 (fish oil) if not eating fish 2-3x/week

**NOT NEEDED:**
- Protein powder (can get from food, but convenient for recovery)
- Creatine (wait until 16+)
- Pre-workout supplements (avoid completely)
- BCAAs (get from whole protein sources)

**ALWAYS CONSULT PEDIATRICIAN BEFORE SUPPLEMENTING**

### **Restaurant/Eating Out Guidelines**

**BEST CHOICES:**
- Grilled chicken or fish dishes
- Pasta with marinara or light sauce
- Rice bowls with lean protein
- Sandwiches/wraps with turkey or chicken
- Stir-fries with rice
- Sushi (cooked options)

**MODIFICATIONS:**
- Ask for dressing on side
- Request grilled instead of fried
- Substitute fries for fruit or rice
- Ask for extra vegetables
- Request light sauce

**AVOID BEFORE COMPETITION:**
- Heavy cream sauces
- Fried foods
- Excessive cheese
- Spicy foods (if sensitive)
- Large portions

### **Hydration Monitoring**

**URINE COLOR CHART:**
- Pale straw yellow = Well hydrated ✓
- Light yellow = Adequate ✓
- Dark yellow = Dehydrated ✗
- Amber/brown = Severely dehydrated ✗✗

**WEIGHT MONITORING:**
- Weigh before and after training
- Every 1 lb lost = 16-24 oz fluid needed
- Should not lose >2% body weight during session

**SIGNS OF DEHYDRATION:**
- Thirst (already behind)
- Dark urine
- Dry mouth
- Headache
- Fatigue
- Dizziness
- Decreased performance

## **TROUBLESHOOTING GUIDE**

### **Common Issues & Solutions**

**ISSUE: Low energy during practice**
- **Cause:** Insufficient pre-practice fuel or hydration
- **Solution:** Increase carbs 2-3 hours before, hydrate better

**ISSUE: Stomach upset during matches**
- **Cause:** Too much food too close to match, high fat/fiber
- **Solution:** Eat 3+ hours before, stick to simple carbs

**ISSUE: Excessive cramping**
- **Cause:** Dehydration, electrolyte imbalance
- **Solution:** Increase sodium intake, drink sports drinks

**ISSUE: Not gaining/maintaining weight**
- **Cause:** Insufficient calories for activity level
- **Solution:** Add calorie-dense snacks, increase portions

**ISSUE: Feeling sluggish next morning**
- **Cause:** Inadequate recovery nutrition or sleep
- **Solution:** Never skip post-training meal, bed by 8:30 PM

**ISSUE: Poor recovery between matches**
- **Cause:** Missing 30-minute window, inadequate hydration
- **Solution:** Immediate recovery shake/snack, aggressive hydration

## **FINAL NOTES**

### **Individualization Important**
- These are evidence-based templates
- Every athlete is unique
- Monitor energy levels, performance, digestion
- Adjust portions based on body weight and activity
- Keep food log for 2 weeks to identify patterns

### **Consistency is Key**
- Follow plan 80-90% of time
- Allow occasional treats (10-20%)
- Don't stress over perfect execution
- Build habits gradually

### **Listen to Your Body**
- If something doesn't feel right, adjust
- Trust your digestion signals
- Don't force food when not hungry
- Eat when genuinely hungry

### **When to Consult Professional**
- Persistent digestive issues
- Unexpected weight loss
- Chronic low energy
- Nutrient deficiencies
- Performance decline despite training

---

**REMEMBER:** Nutrition is fuel for performance. The better the fuel, the better the performance. Make it a priority equal to training!
