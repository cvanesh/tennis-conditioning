### WARM-UP EXERCISES

#### **Dynamic Stretching**

**üé• Walking Lunges**
- [Video: Walking Lunge Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=walking+lunge+proper+form)
- Step forward into lunge, knee doesn't pass toes

### CORE EXERCISES

**üé• Forearm Plank**
- [Video: Plank Exercise Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=forearm+plank+proper+form)
- Elbows under shoulders
- Body in straight line
- Don't let hips sag or pike up
- Engage core and glutes

**üé• Side Plank**
- [Video: Side Plank Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=side+plank+exercise+tutorial)
- Start from knees (easier) or feet (harder)
- Stack hips vertically
- Don't let hips drop
- Engage obliques

**üé• Dead Bug**
- [Video: Dead Bug Exercise](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=dead+bug+core+exercise+tutorial)
- Lie on back, arms up, knees at 90¬∞
- Lower opposite arm and leg
- Keep lower back pressed to floor
- Slow and controlled

**üé• Superman / Prone Hold**
- [Video: Superman Exercise](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=superman+exercise+tutorial)
- Lie face down
- Lift chest and legs simultaneously
- Squeeze glutes and lower back

- Lift shoulders and legs

- Arms extended overhead

**üé• Mountain Climbers**
- [Video: Mountain Climbers Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=mountain+climbers+exercise+tutorial)

- Keep hips level
- Controlled movement (not sloppy)

**üé• Russian Twists**
- [Video: Russian Twist Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=russian+twist+exercise+tutorial)

### PLYOMETRIC EXERCISES

**üé• Two-Foot Jumps**
- [Video: Vertical Jump Technique](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=vertical+jump+technique+tutorial)
- Start in athletic stance
- Swing arms, explode up
- Land softly (toes-ball-heel)
- Minimize ground contact time

**üé• Box Jumps**
- [Video: Box Jump Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=box+jump+proper+form+tutorial)
- Face box in athletic stance
- Jump onto box, land softly
- Stand fully at top
- **STEP DOWN** between reps (don't jump down)

**üé• Broad Jumps**
- [Video: Broad Jump Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=broad+jump+technique+tutorial)
- Jump for distance, not height
- Swing arms back, then forward
- Land in squat position
- Stick landing (don't fall back)

**üé• Lateral Bounds**
- [Video: Lateral Bound Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=lateral+bound+plyometric+tutorial)
- Jump side to side
- Push off outside leg
- Land on opposite leg
- Stick landing before next rep

**üé• Split Squat Jumps**
- [Video: Split Squat Jump Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=split+squat+jump+tutorial)
- Start in lunge position

**üé• Forward Bounds / Bounding**

- Focus on distance per bound

- Powerful arm swing

**üé• Depth Jumps**
- [Video: Depth Jump Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=depth+jump+plyometric+tutorial)
- Step off box
- Land and immediately jump
- Minimize ground contact time
- Advanced - master box jumps first

**üé• Skater Jumps**
- [Video: Skater Jump Tutorial](https://www.youtube.com/results?search_query=skater+jumps+exercise+tutorial)
- Lateral jump from leg to leg

## ‚ö†Ô∏è IMPORTANT SAFETY NOTES

### **When to Watch Demonstration Videos:**
- **ALWAYS** before attempting a new exercise
- If you're unsure about form on any exercise
- Before progressing to harder variations
- If you feel pain (something might be wrong with technique)

### **How to Use Video Demonstrations:**
1. **Watch the ENTIRE video first** (don't skip ahead)
2. **Pay attention to**:
   - Starting position
   - Movement path
   - Common mistakes section
   - Breathing pattern
3. **Practice 3-5 reps slowly** while watching video
4. **Then perform without video** but focus on form

### **Red Flags - Stop Immediately If:**
- Sharp or shooting pain (not muscle burn)
- Joint pain (knees, shoulders, back, etc.)
- Dizziness or lightheadedness
- Form completely breaks down despite rest

## üí™ PARENT/COACH OBSERVATION GUIDE

### **What to Look For:**

**General Movement Quality:**
- Smooth, controlled motion (not jerky)
- Symmetrical movement (both sides look the same)
- Proper breathing (not holding breath)
- Good balance and stability

**Exercise-Specific Cues:**

**Lower Body:**
- Knees stay aligned (don't collapse inward)
- Equal weight distribution
- Landing mechanics (soft, controlled)

**Core:**
- No excessive arching in lower back
- Hips stay level (no rotation)
- Maintains neutral spine

**Upper Body:**
- Shoulders stay down (away from ears)
- No shrugging during exercises
- Scapular control (shoulder blades moving properly)

### **Providing Feedback:**
‚úì "Keep your chest up during squats"
‚úì "Your hips are dropping - engage your core more"
‚úì "Great job on controlling that landing!"
‚úó Avoid: "You're doing it wrong" (be specific)

# PROGRAM OVERVIEW

**Target Audience:** 12-year-old male tennis player with solid stroke technique but minimal physical conditioning background

**Program Duration:** 8 weeks

**Training Frequency:** 4 sessions per week (Monday, Tuesday, Thursday, Friday)

**Session Duration:** 30-45 minutes

**Program Philosophy:** This program follows Long-Term Athletic Development (LTAD) principles for the "Train to Train" stage (ages 12-16), focusing on building foundational fitness, movement quality, and injury prevention while maintaining enjoyment and sustainable progress.

---

## Equipment Required

- Tennis court or 15x15 yard space
- 5-6 cones (12-14 inches tall)
- 1 medicine ball (1-2 kg / 2-4 lbs to start)
- Resistance band (light to medium)
- Yoga mat or towel
- Timer/stopwatch
- Water bottle
- **Smartphone or tablet for video demonstrations**

---

## Weekly Structure

**Monday:** Speed, Agility & Coordination  
**Tuesday:** Core Strength & Stability  
**Thursday:** Lower Body Power & Plyometrics  
**Friday:** Upper Body Strength & Endurance

**Rest Days:** Wednesday, Saturday, Sunday (on-court tennis training can occur on these days)
